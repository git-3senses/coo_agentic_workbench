# NPA Approvals - Who, When, and Why in NPA Lifecycle

**Version**: 1.0
**Effective Date**: February 2026
**Purpose**: Define comprehensive approval framework for New Product Approval lifecycle

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document provides the definitive approval framework for all NPA submissions, detailing who approves, when approvals are required, why specific approvals are necessary, and the complete approval workflow across all NPA types and classifications.

---

## 1. APPROVAL FRAMEWORK OVERVIEW

### 1.1 Approval Hierarchy

```
BOARD LEVEL
├── Board Risk Committee (Ultimate oversight)
├── Board Audit Committee (Control framework)
└── Full Board (Strategic decisions)

EXECUTIVE LEVEL
├── CEO (Final executive authority)
├── Group COO (Operational oversight)
├── Group CRO (Risk oversight)
└── Group CCO (Compliance oversight)

COMMITTEE LEVEL
├── Product Approval Committee (PAC) - NTG products
├── Group Executive Committee (Major strategic decisions)
├── Group Risk Committee (Risk approval)
└── Group Operational Risk Committee (Operational approval)

FUNCTIONAL LEVEL
├── Group Product Head (Product governance)
├── Business Unit Head (Business approval)
├── Department Heads (Functional approval)
└── Sign-off Parties (Technical approval)
```

### 1.2 Approval Authority Matrix

| Authority Level | Decision Scope | NPA Types | Override Capability |
|----------------|----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Board** | Strategic direction, major risk | High-impact NTG | Ultimate authority |
| **CEO** | Executive decisions, escalations | Complex NTG, regulatory issues | Board only |
| **Group COO** | Operational approval, final sign-off | All NPAs | CEO only |
| **PAC** | New-to-Group product approval | All NTG products | Group COO/CEO |
| **Group Product Head** | Product strategy alignment | All NPAs | Group COO |
| **BU Head** | Business unit alignment | Unit-specific NPAs | Group Product Head |

### 1.3 Approval Timeline Framework

| NPA Type | Standard Timeline | Fast-Track Timeline | Emergency Timeline |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Full NPA (NTG)** | 12-16 weeks | 8-10 weeks | 4-6 weeks |
| **Full NPA (Variation)** | 8-12 weeks | 6-8 weeks | 3-4 weeks |
| **NPA Lite** | 4-6 weeks | 3-4 weeks | 1-2 weeks |

## 2. CORE APPROVAL PARTIES - WHO APPROVES

### 2.1 Mandatory Sign-Off Parties (All NPAs)

#### 2.1.1 Risk Management Group
**WHO**: Group Chief Risk Officer and designated teams
**ROLE**: Risk assessment and mitigation validation

##### Market & Liquidity Risk Team
- **WHO**: Head of Market & Liquidity Risk + Senior Risk Managers
- **WHEN**: All trading products, market-sensitive products
- **WHY**: Market risk exposure assessment, liquidity impact evaluation
- **AUTHORITY**: Risk limit approval, model validation sign-off
- **TIMELINE**: 5-7 business days

##### Credit Risk Team
- **WHO**: Head of Credit Risk + Portfolio Risk Managers
- **WHEN**: Products with credit exposure, counterparty risk
- **WHY**: Credit risk assessment, portfolio impact analysis
- **AUTHORITY**: Credit limit approval, collateral framework validation
- **TIMELINE**: 5-7 business days

#### 2.1.2 Technology & Operations
**WHO**: Group Chief Technology Officer and Operations heads
**ROLE**: Technical feasibility and operational readiness validation

##### Technology Assessment
- **WHO**: Head of Technology Architecture + Platform leads
- **WHEN**: All products requiring system changes/enhancements
- **WHY**: Technical feasibility, infrastructure impact, security assessment
- **AUTHORITY**: System development approval, architecture validation
- **TIMELINE**: 7-10 business days

##### Operations Assessment
- **WHO**: Head of Operations + Process owners
- **WHEN**: All products with operational impact
- **WHY**: Operational feasibility, process impact, resource requirements
- **AUTHORITY**: Operational process approval, resource allocation
- **TIMELINE**: 5-7 business days

#### 2.1.3 Legal, Compliance & Secretariat
**WHO**: General Counsel, Chief Compliance Officer, Company Secretary
**ROLE**: Legal compliance and regulatory adherence validation

##### Legal Review
- **WHO**: General Counsel + Legal specialists
- **WHEN**: All products requiring legal documentation/agreements
- **WHY**: Legal risk assessment, documentation adequacy, enforceability
- **AUTHORITY**: Legal documentation approval, external counsel engagement
- **TIMELINE**: 7-10 business days

##### Compliance Review
- **WHO**: Chief Compliance Officer + Compliance specialists
- **WHEN**: All products with regulatory implications
- **WHY**: Regulatory compliance, AML/sanctions compliance, licensing
- **AUTHORITY**: Regulatory approach approval, compliance framework validation
- **TIMELINE**: 7-10 business days

#### 2.1.4 Finance
**WHO**: Group Chief Financial Officer and finance teams
**ROLE**: Financial impact and accounting treatment validation

##### Financial Analysis
- **WHO**: Head of Finance + Financial controllers
- **WHEN**: All products with financial/accounting impact
- **WHY**: Accounting treatment, tax implications, financial control
- **AUTHORITY**: Accounting method approval, financial control validation
- **TIMELINE**: 5-7 business days

### 2.2 Special Approval Authorities

#### 2.2.1 Product Approval Committee (PAC)
**WHO**: Senior executives from key functions
**COMPOSITION**:
- Group COO (Chair)
- Group CRO (Risk oversight)
- Group CCO (Compliance oversight)
- Group CFO (Financial oversight)
- Relevant Business Head (Business sponsorship)

**WHEN**: ALL New-to-Group (NTG) products - NO EXCEPTIONS
**WHY**: Strategic oversight, enterprise risk management, resource allocation
**AUTHORITY**: Final approval for NTG products, strategic direction setting
**TIMELINE**: Monthly meetings, special meetings for urgent NPAs

##### PAC Approval Process:
1. **NPA Presentation** (30-45 minutes)
2. **Executive Discussion** (15-30 minutes)
3. **Decision/Conditions** (Immediate or deferred)
4. **Post-Meeting Documentation** (Within 24 hours)

#### 2.2.2 CEO Approval
**WHO**: Chief Executive Officer
**WHEN**:
- High-impact NTG products (>$100M revenue potential)
- Regulatory-sensitive products
- Strategic partnership products
- PAC escalations
- Crisis/emergency NPAs

**WHY**: Ultimate executive accountability, strategic alignment, stakeholder management
**AUTHORITY**: Final executive decision, board recommendation
**TIMELINE**: 3-5 business days from escalation

#### 2.2.3 Board Approval
**WHO**: Board of Directors/Board Risk Committee
**WHEN**:
- Products affecting bank strategy
- Products with material risk exposure (>$500M)
- Products requiring regulatory approval
- Products affecting bank license/charter

**WHY**: Fiduciary responsibility, shareholder protection, regulatory compliance
**AUTHORITY**: Ultimate approval authority
**TIMELINE**: Next scheduled board meeting or special meeting

## 3. APPROVAL WORKFLOWS BY NPA TYPE

### 3.1 New-to-Group (NTG) Products
**MANDATORY PATH**: Most comprehensive approval process

#### Stage 1: Initial Validation (Weeks 1-2)
```
Business Unit Head → Group Product Head → Group COO (Initial Approval to Proceed)
```

#### Stage 2: Technical Assessment (Weeks 3-6)
```
Parallel Review Process:
├── Risk Management Group (Market, Credit, Operational)
├── Technology & Operations (Systems, Processes)
├── Legal, Compliance & Secretariat (Regulatory, Legal)
└── Finance (Accounting, Tax, Capital)
```

#### Stage 3: PAC Approval (Weeks 7-8)
```
PAC Presentation → PAC Decision → Conditions/Requirements → Final PAC Approval
```

#### Stage 4: Implementation Approval (Weeks 9-12)
```
Implementation Plan → Resource Allocation → Go-Live Approval → Group COO Sign-off
```

#### Escalation Triggers for NTG:
- **CEO Approval Required**:
  - Revenue potential >$100M annually
  - Regulatory approval required
  - New market entry
  - Strategic partnerships

- **Board Approval Required**:
  - Risk exposure >$500M
  - License/charter implications
  - Material strategy change
  - Regulatory mandate

### 3.2 Variation Products
**PATH**: Streamlined approval based on materiality

#### Material Variations (Full NPA Path):
```
Stage 1: Classification Validation (Week 1)
├── Product Manager Assessment
├── Business Head Validation
└── Group Product Head Confirmation

Stage 2: Technical Review (Weeks 2-4)
├── Risk Assessment (focused on changes)
├── Operational Impact (change assessment)
├── Legal/Compliance (delta review)
└── Finance (impact analysis)

Stage 3: Final Approval (Week 5-6)
├── Business Head Approval
├── Group Product Head Approval
└── Group COO Sign-off
```

#### Minor Variations (NPA Lite Path):
```
Stage 1: Simplified Assessment (Week 1)
├── Change Impact Analysis
└── Risk Delta Assessment

Stage 2: Streamlined Review (Week 2-3)
├── Risk Review (focused)
├── Compliance Check (regulatory impact)
└── Operational Validation (process impact)

Stage 3: Approval (Week 4)
├── Business Head Approval
└── Group Product Head Sign-off
```

### 3.3 Existing Products
**PATH**: Minimal approval process with validation focus

#### Standard Existing Products:
```
Week 1: Similarity Validation
├── Product Comparison
├── Risk Profile Confirmation
└── Regulatory Status Check

Week 2: Operational Readiness
├── System Capability Check
├── Process Validation
└── Resource Confirmation

Week 3: Final Approval
├── Business Head Approval
└── Implementation Authorization
```

### 3.4 Bundling Products
**PATH**: Combination-specific approval process

#### Bundling Approval Workflow:
```
Week 1-2: Component Validation
├── Individual Product Status Check
├── Combination Risk Assessment
└── Legal Structure Review

Week 3-4: Integration Review
├── Operational Integration Assessment
├── Technology Integration Review
├── Customer Experience Validation
└── Revenue Allocation Approval

Week 5: Final Approval
├── Bundling Team Approval
├── Business Head Sign-off
└── Group Product Head Authorization
```

### 3.5 Evergreen Products
**PATH**: Automated approval with parameter validation

#### Evergreen Approval Process:
```
Week 1: Parameter Validation
├── Automated Compliance Check
├── Parameter Range Validation
└── Deviation Detection

Week 2: Final Authorization (if no deviations)
├── Automated Business Approval
└── System-Generated Authorization

Deviation Path (if deviations detected):
├── Manual Review Required
├── Classification Reassessment
└── Appropriate NPA Process
```

## 4. APPROVAL TIMING AND SEQUENCING

### 4.1 Approval Sequence Rules

#### Sequential Dependencies:
1. **Business Case Approval** → Technical Assessment
2. **Risk Assessment** → Compliance Review
3. **Legal Documentation** → Implementation Planning
4. **PAC Approval** → Resource Allocation
5. **Final Technical Validation** → Go-Live Authorization

#### Parallel Processing:
- **Risk, Compliance, Legal, Finance** can review simultaneously
- **System and Operations** assessment can run in parallel
- **Documentation preparation** can occur during technical review

### 4.2 Critical Path Management

#### Critical Path Items (Cannot be parallelized):
1. **Initial Business Approval** (Gate 1)
2. **PAC Approval** (Gate 2 - NTG only)
3. **Final Technical Validation** (Gate 3)
4. **Group COO Sign-off** (Gate 4)

#### Bottleneck Management:
- **External Legal Opinions**: Start early, run in parallel
- **Regulatory Consultations**: Initiate during early stages
- **Model Validation**: Begin with technical assessment
- **System Development**: Start after technical approval

### 4.3 Approval Timeline Optimization

#### Fast-Track Eligibility:
- **Business Urgency**: Market opportunity, competitive response
- **Regulatory Deadline**: Compliance-driven implementation
- **Customer Commitment**: Existing customer commitment
- **Strategic Priority**: CEO/Board strategic initiative

#### Fast-Track Process Modifications:
- **Parallel Reviews**: All technical reviews simultaneous
- **Daily Check-ins**: Accelerated communication
- **Executive Escalation**: Direct access to senior approvers
- **Resource Priority**: Dedicated review resources

#### Emergency Approval Process:
- **Crisis Response**: Market crisis, regulatory emergency
- **Executive Override**: CEO/Group COO expedited decision
- **Post-Approval Validation**: Full review completed post-launch
- **Risk Monitoring**: Enhanced monitoring during emergency implementation

## 5. APPROVAL CONDITIONS AND REQUIREMENTS

### 5.1 Standard Approval Conditions

#### Universal Conditions (All NPAs):
- **Risk Limits**: Adherence to approved risk parameters
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Full regulatory adherence
- **System Readiness**: Technology infrastructure prepared
- **Operational Readiness**: Processes and resources ready
- **Legal Documentation**: All legal requirements completed
- **Staff Training**: Relevant staff trained and competent

#### NTG-Specific Conditions:
- **PAC Conditions**: All PAC requirements satisfied
- **PIR Commitment**: Post-Implementation Review at 6 months
- **Monitoring Framework**: Enhanced monitoring for first 12 months
- **Success Metrics**: Achievement of defined success criteria
- **Escalation Procedures**: Clear escalation for issues/deviations

### 5.2 Conditional Approvals

#### Approval with Conditions:
- **Implementation Conditions**: Specific requirements before go-live
- **Ongoing Conditions**: Requirements during operation
- **Review Conditions**: Mandatory review points
- **Performance Conditions**: Achievement of specific metrics

#### Condition Monitoring:
- **Condition Owner**: Responsible party for each condition
- **Monitoring Frequency**: Daily, weekly, monthly checks
- **Reporting Requirements**: Regular status updates
- **Escalation Triggers**: Condition violation responses

### 5.3 Approval Revocation

#### Revocation Triggers:
- **Material Change**: Significant deviation from approved parameters
- **Regulatory Change**: New regulatory requirements affecting product
- **Risk Materialization**: Unexpected risk events
- **Performance Failure**: Failure to meet approved metrics

#### Revocation Process:
1. **Trigger Identification** (Any stakeholder)
2. **Assessment Committee** (Original approvers)
3. **Revocation Decision** (Group COO/PAC)
4. **Wind-down Plan** (Business unit)
5. **Stakeholder Notification** (All affected parties)

## 8. ESCALATION AND EXCEPTION MANAGEMENT

### 8.1 Approval Escalation Framework

#### Escalation Triggers:
- **Timeline Delays**: Approval process behind schedule
- **Disagreement**: Conflicts between approvers
- **Resource Constraints**: Insufficient review resources
- **External Pressures**: Customer/market pressures
- **Regulatory Issues**: Regulatory concerns or changes

#### Escalation Process:
```
Level 1: Department Head Resolution (2 days)
    ├── Issue identification and initial resolution attempt
    └── Stakeholder notification and communication

Level 2: Business Unit Head Escalation (3 days)
    ├── Cross-functional issue resolution
    └── Resource reallocation if needed

Level 3: Group Product Head Intervention (2 days)
    ├── Strategic decision and direction
    └── Executive stakeholder engagement

Level 4: Group COO Decision (1 day)
    ├── Final operational decision
    └── CEO notification if needed

Level 5: CEO Resolution (Same day)
    ├── Executive decision and direction
    └── Board notification if material
```

### 8.2 Exception Management

#### Exception Types:
- **Process Exceptions**: Deviations from standard approval process
- **Timeline Exceptions**: Approval timeline modifications
- **Authority Exceptions**: Alternative approval authorities
- **Documentation Exceptions**: Alternative documentation requirements

#### Exception Approval:
- **Minor Exceptions**: Business Head approval
- **Major Exceptions**: Group Product Head approval
- **Critical Exceptions**: Group COO approval
- **Strategic Exceptions**: CEO approval
